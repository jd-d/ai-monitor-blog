AI Chip Demand Strains Semiconductor Supply Amid Policy Risks

Trigger risk: Elevated Supply Update SEMI SUPPLY Confidence: Medium NSI: 47.5

What changed

Demand for high-performance chips to run generative AI models is surging. Deloitte forecasts that generative-AI chip revenue could exceed $150 billion in 2025, but advanced nodes make up less than 0.2 % of wafer capacity.

Context

To meet demand, the EU Chips Act aims to double Europe’s small manufacturing share to 20 % and mobilise €43 billion, while the U.S. Chips Act offers $39 billion in subsidies but faces political headwinds. Export controls introduced in January 2025 allow chip exports to allies but restrict sales to countries of concern.

Why it matters

  • Tight supply of advanced chips could delay AI deployments or inflate costs.
  • Policy incentives may create new regional champions but cannot quickly solve capacity constraints.
  • Geopolitical restrictions and uncertain subsidies add downside risk.

Watch next

  • Follow announcements of new fabs and advanced packaging capacity.
  • Monitor changes to Chips Act funding and export-control policies.
Sources: Deloitte, EETimes
