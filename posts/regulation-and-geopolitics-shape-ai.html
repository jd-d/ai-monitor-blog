28 September 2025

Regulation and Geopolitics Reshape AI Landscape

Sustainability state: Watch Policy Update POLICY GEOPOLITICS Confidence: Medium NSI: 47.5

What changed

The EU AI Act took effect, creating a tiered, risk-based framework that bans eight unacceptable AI practices. Meanwhile, the EU Chips Act mobilises €43 billion to boost Europe’s semiconductor share to 20 %. In the U.S., the Chips Act’s $39 billion subsidy program faces potential repeal, and new export controls restrict AI chip sales to countries of concern.

Context

Governments worldwide are increasingly shaping AI through both incentives and restrictions. The EU is pushing for technological sovereignty, while U.S. policy is in flux and aimed at protecting national security. These policies affect where and how companies invest in AI hardware and software.

Why it matters

  • Clear rules such as the EU AI Act provide predictability but may raise compliance costs.
  • Generous subsidies can attract investment but political reversals introduce uncertainty.
  • Export controls limit access to advanced chips in some countries, influencing global competition.

Watch next

  • Follow implementation of the AI Act and how it affects developers and users.
  • Monitor the political debate around the U.S. Chips Act and any new export-control measures.
Sources: EU Digital Strategy, Reuters
