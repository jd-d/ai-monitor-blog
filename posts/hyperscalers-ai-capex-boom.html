28 September 2025

Hyperscalers Expand AI Capex Amid $7 Trillion Buildout

Sustainability state: Elevated Investment Update AI CAPEX Confidence: Medium NSI: 55.0

What changed

Major cloud providers massively increased spending on AI-dedicated data centers, with 2024 investment around $210 billion and Microsoft planning $80 billion in FY2025. Analysts project that generative AI will require $5–7 trillion in new data-center investment by 2030.

Context

Hyperscalers are building out capacity to meet surging demand for generative AI. AI workloads could account for 70 % of total data-center demand. Yet current AI revenues are only about $10 billion, raising questions about capital coverage.

Why it matters

  • Exploding capex shows investor confidence in AI but heightens financial risk if revenue growth lags.
  • The expected trillions in investment signal long-term commitment to AI infrastructure.
  • Capex guidance changes or financing cuts could signal a shift in sentiment.

Watch next

  • Look for updated capex guidance from hyperscalers and whether revenue growth keeps pace.
  • Track alternative financing strategies or joint ventures that share infrastructure costs.
Sources: Newmark 2025 Data Center Outlook, Brown Advisory
