Power Constraints Threaten AI Data-Center Growth

Trigger risk: Critical Power Update POWER GRID Confidence: Medium NSI: 32.5

What changed

AI-driven data-center electricity demand is projected to rise from 4 GW today to 123 GW by 2035. Utilities such as Dominion Energy note that data centers already consume a quarter of regional power and orders could double capacity by 2028.

Context

The surge in AI workloads is pushing power demand far beyond current grid capacity. In Europe, data centers account for up to 80 % of electricity consumption in Dublin and connections can take years.

Why it matters

  • Power constraints could become the main bottleneck for AI scalability.
  • Failure to secure power purchase agreements may delay or cancel planned data-center projects.
  • Rising electricity costs and delays could pressure margins and shift investment to regions with better grid access.

Watch next

  • Track public-private power deals and interconnect approvals to assess whether supply keeps up with demand.
  • Monitor regulatory incentives for renewable generation and grid upgrades.
Sources: Deloitte, Ember
