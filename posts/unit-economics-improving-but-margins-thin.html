AI Unit Costs Plunge While Margins Remain Thin

Sustainability state: Watch Cost Update UNIT ECONOMICS Confidence: High NSI: 62.5

What changed

Between 2021 and 2024, the cost to generate one million tokens fell from about $60 to $0.06, and overall inference costs dropped more than 280-fold. AI servers now run at 80–90 % utilisation.

Context

Improved GPUs, software optimisation and model quantisation drive down per-token costs. However, each query still consumes considerable energy and compute resources, keeping gross margins around 50–60 %.

Why it matters

  • Rapid cost declines improve the economics of inference and lower barriers to adoption.
  • High utilisation shows infrastructure is being used efficiently.
  • Margins remain thin, so further efficiency gains are necessary for profitability.

Watch next

  • Track the pace of cost reductions from new hardware and software techniques.
  • Watch utilisation and energy efficiency metrics across hyperscaler fleets.
Sources: a16z, Data Center Frontier
